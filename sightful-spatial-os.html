EA Onboarding Usability Research

This preliminary research explored how people experience the onboarding flow during their first session. The goal was to understand whether new users could independently complete setup and engage with key modules, and to identify friction points in the early experience.

UX Researcher Methods: Usability Testing · Interviews · SUS · NASA TLX Participants: 10 target users
Overview

Participants completed the full onboarding process with minimal intervention. After setup, they performed a structured multitasking task designed to reflect real-world usage.

  • Observe interaction with onboarding modules
  • Assess first-time clarity and independence
  • Measure usability and perceived cognitive load
Study design
  • Participants completed onboarding modules (Fitting, Reorient, Canvas Resize)
  • Performed structured multitasking across multiple windows
  • Completed SUS (usability) and NASA TLX (cognitive load)
  • Participated in a follow-up qualitative interview
Onboarding UT
Key findings

Most participants were able to complete the structured task. However, several usability and clarity issues emerged during onboarding.

  • Some modules were missed or ignored during first exposure
  • Module notifications were not always visually salient
  • Users sometimes misunderstood the purpose of certain modules
  • Interaction patterns were not always intuitive (e.g., clickable bubbles)
Interaction friction themes
  • Difficulty recognizing module notifications
  • Unclear feedback after calibration actions
  • Confusion about how to launch modules
  • Desire to adjust workspace before modules appeared
  • Requests for additional guidance on brightness, sound, and system controls
Fitting module engagement

Engagement varied across participants. While many completed the fitting steps, some skipped or partially completed them. This revealed opportunities to strengthen visibility and reinforce the value of calibration.

Quantitative findings

In addition to qualitative usability insights, we collected standardized usability and workload metrics to quantify overall product performance.

System Usability Scale (SUS)

The calculated SUS score was 73.89. On a 0–100 scale, this score is considered above average and indicates good overall usability. It suggests that most users were able to complete tasks with relative confidence and minimal assistance.

NASA TLX (Cognitive Load)

The average NASA TLX score across participants was 43.33. On a 0–100 scale, this reflects a medium level of perceived task load.

NASA TLX measures subjective mental workload across multiple dimensions, including effort, performance, and frustration. A score in this range suggests that while the product required some cognitive effort, it did not create excessive stress or overload during the task.

Impact

The study clarified which onboarding elements were intuitive versus which required stronger guidance, clearer feedback, or improved design.

  • Identified missed modules and notification visibility issues
  • Highlighted calibration clarity gaps
  • Resulted in a complete change to the onboarding process and UI design
