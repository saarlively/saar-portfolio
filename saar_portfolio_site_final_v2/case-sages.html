AI Behavior & Trust Research (Public-safe)

Mixed-method UX research to define trust thresholds, data comfort, and experience principles for an AI platform focused on reflection and everyday decision-making.

Role: UX Research & Product Experience Methods: Interviews · Surveys · Synthesis Notes: Public-safe summary
Context

Sages is an early-stage AI platform exploring how people reflect, learn, and make decisions with support from adaptive systems. My work focused on aligning user needs, trust thresholds, and experience strategy into an actionable MVP direction.

My role
  • Led mixed-method UX research (interviews, surveys, synthesis)
  • Translated insights into frameworks and product experience direction
  • Partnered with design/engineering leadership to validate 0→1 flows
What we studied
  • When people seek help vs. self-reflect
  • Comfort with data sharing and perceived privacy tradeoffs
  • Trust requirements for an AI companion experience
  • What “self-improvement” means in everyday life
Key insights (public-safe)
  • Users wanted clarity and control before “delight” — transparency increased adoption intent
  • Trust was contextual: sensitivity varied by domain and perceived benefit
  • People preferred systems that showed their reasoning and allowed corrections
Impact
  • Defined experience principles for trust-forward onboarding
  • Shaped prioritization of early MVP flows and feedback loops
  • Created a shared language for how the system should behave (e.g., when to ask, when to suggest, when to stay quiet)
